

Find & clear inactive accounts across your Entra tenant
Tenant Guard surfaces members and guests with no interactive or non-interactive sign-in beyond your chosen window, and lets an administrator remove them — directly against Microsoft Graph, with no backend.
Sign in with Microsoft
WHAT THIS REQUESTS (DELEGATED, ADMIN-CONSENTED)
	•	● User.Read.All & AuditLog.Read.All — read users and their sign-in activity ● Directory.Read.All — read tenant license state (required for sign-in activity) ● Device.Read.All — read registered devices and their last sign-in ● IdentityRiskyUser.Read.All, Policy.Read.All, UserAuthenticationMethod.Read.All, ServiceHealth.Read.All — security views (risky users, Conditional Access, MFA, service health) ● BitlockerKey.ReadBasic.All, DeviceLocalCredential.ReadBasic.All & DeviceManagementManagedDevices.Read.All — compliance & antivirus views: BitLocker recovery-key and Windows LAPS metadata (no key/password material), Intune encryption status, Defender antivirus health, and active-malware reporting ● SecurityEvents.Read.All & Application.Read.All — advanced security views: Microsoft Secure Score, and Entra app registration credential expiry (privileged accounts, legacy auth and OAuth consents reuse the scopes above) ● User.ReadWrite.All — delete selected user accounts (soft-delete, recoverable 30 days) ● Directory.AccessAsUser.All — requested only at the moment you delete a device (Graph has no narrower delegated permission for that operation); permanent, no recovery ● Needs an Entra ID P1/P2 license. Deleting users needs User Administrator or Global Administrator; deleting devices needs Cloud Device Administrator, Intune Administrator, or Global Administrator.
